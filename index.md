### 1. The Geometry of Life: How Physical Laws Dictate Biological Patterns
**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Tags:** Morphogenesis, Physics, Phase Transitions

For decades, Turing's reaction-diffusion theory has explained *how* patterns form, but not *how many* form. In our latest research, we derived a universal law: **Geometric Quantization**. We show that the number of patterns (fingers, stripes, segments) is quantized by the ratio of system size to interaction range, much like quantum states in a box.

### 2. Growing Pains: The Physics of Pattern Insertion in Developing Organs
**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Tags:** Developmental Biology, Growth, Simulation

Biological organisms grow, but patterns don't just stretch. Our dynamic growth simulations reveal a mechanism of **Pattern Insertion via Phase Transitions**. We show how systems remain metastable in discrete modes and undergo sudden jumps to insert new patterns as space becomes available, explaining sequential segmentation in embryos.

### 3. The Physics of Evolution: Why Nature Loves Phase Transitions
**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Tags:** Evolution, Robustness, Saltationism

We explore the **Phase Diagram of Morphology**, showing how "plateaus" provide evolutionary robustness (Canalization) while phase boundaries enable **Saltational Evolution** (macro-mutations).
