## Experiment 1: DC-396 — AI Architecture Φ Comparison

**Start Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Duration:** 12 months  
**Budget:** $45K  
**Risk Level:** Minimal (no human subjects)

### Objectives

Compare Φ (integrated information) values across different AI architectures to test ITLCT prediction:

**Prediction:** Transformer < RNN < GWT < QNN (Φ_max increases with architectural irreducibility)

### Methods

- **AI Models:** 5 architectures (Transformer, RNN, LSTM, GWT-inspired, hybrid)
- **Φ Measurement:** Φ_NN approximator (accuracy >92%)
- **Tasks:** Standard AI benchmarks + consciousness-specific tasks
- **Analysis:** Cross-architecture Φ comparison, correlation with task performance

### Ethics

- No human subjects
- AI systems only (no rights concerns at this stage)
- Open source all code and data

### Expected Outcomes

- Validate AI Φ architecture limits
- Inform AI safety consciousness detection
- Guide AI architecture design for consciousness safety

## Experiment 2: DC-392 — Anesthesia Critical Point Φ Phase Transition

**Start Date:** 2026-05-01  
**Duration:** 18 months  
**Budget:** $80K  
**Risk Level:** Minimal (observational, no intervention)

### Objectives

Test ITLCT prediction that consciousness shows critical phase transition at anesthesia induction:

**Predictions:**
- Φ_c ≈ 0.35 (consciousness threshold)
- Critical slowing down near Φ_c (recovery time ↑)
- Critical exponent β ≈ 0.5 (mean field)

### Methods

- **Participants:** 60 healthy adults (18-35 years)
- **Procedure:** Observational study during scheduled surgery
- **Measurements:**
  - EEG (Φ estimation via PCI/LZc)
  - Behavioral responsiveness (command following)
  - Anesthesia depth (BIS monitor)
- **Analysis:** Critical point detection, exponent estimation

### Ethics

- **Risk:** Minimal (observational only, no intervention)
- **Consent:** Written informed consent required
- **Privacy:** De-identified data, secure storage
- **Benefit:** Improved consciousness monitoring during surgery

### Inclusion Criteria

- Age 18-35
- Scheduled surgery requiring general anesthesia
- No neurological/psychiatric history
- Fluent in task language

### Exclusion Criteria

- Neurological disorders
- Psychiatric conditions
- Substance abuse
- Pregnancy

### Expected Outcomes

- Validate consciousness critical point theory
- Improve intraoperative consciousness monitoring
- Inform anesthesia dosing protocols

## Experiment 3: DC-393 — Meditation Φ Enhancement RCT

**Start Date:** 2026-05-01  
**Duration:** 24 months  
**Budget:** $120K  
**Risk Level:** Minimal (meditation is low-risk)

### Objectives

Test ITLCT prediction that meditation training increases Φ:

**Predictions:**
- 8-week meditation → Φ ↑ 10-20%
- Effects persist at 6-month follow-up (>10% baseline)
- Dose-response relationship (practice time ↔ Φ increase)

### Methods

- **Design:** Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT)
- **Participants:** 200 adults (18-60 years)
- **Groups:**
  - Meditation group (n=100): 8-week MBSR program
  - Active control (n=50): Health education
  - Waitlist control (n=50): No intervention
- **Measurements:**
  - Φ (EEG-based estimation)
  - fMRI (subset, neural correlates)
  - Behavioral (creativity, attention, well-being)
  - Follow-up: 6 months post-intervention

### Ethics

- **Risk:** Minimal (meditation is generally safe)
- **Consent:** Written informed consent required
- **Privacy:** De-identified data, secure storage
- **Benefit:** Mental health improvements, stress reduction

### Inclusion Criteria

- Age 18-60
- No prior meditation experience (>1 hour total)
- Willing to commit to 8-week program
- Fluent in task language

### Exclusion Criteria

- Current meditation practice
- Psychiatric conditions (unstable)
- Neurological disorders
- Substance abuse

### Expected Outcomes

- Validate meditation-Φ link
- Inform mental health interventions
- Guide consciousness training protocols

- **Privacy:** De-identified data, secure storage
- **Benefit:** Improved decision-making, metacognition

### Training Components

- **A (Autonomy):** Self-directed goal setting, executive function training
- **Φ (Consciousness):** Meditation, attention training

### Expected Outcomes

- Validate F-value trainability

## Experiment 5: DC-404 — σ₃ Temperature Dependence

**Start Date:** 2026-07-01  
**Duration:** 18 months  
**Budget:** $100K  
**Risk Level:** Minimal (temperature manipulation within safe range)

### Objectives

Test ITLCT prediction that life-consciousness-entropy coupling (σ₃) shows temperature dependence:

**Predictions:**
- σ₃(T) shows peak at 295K ± 15K (carbon-based life optimum)
- Half-maximum width ≈ 30K
- Cross-species scaling: σ₃ ∝ M_body^(-0.25)

### Methods

- **Subjects:** Multiple species (human, non-human primate, rodent, fish, insect)
- **Procedure:** Controlled temperature manipulation (within ethical limits)
- **Measurements:**
  - Metabolic rate (oxygen consumption)
  - Φ (consciousness estimation)
  - L-value (life measure)
  - Entropy production (calorimetry)
- **Analysis:** σ₃(T) curve fitting, cross-species scaling

### Ethics

- **Risk:** Minimal (temperature within physiological range)
- **Animal Ethics:** IACUC approval required for animal studies
- **Consent:** Human consent required
- **Welfare:** All procedures within ethical guidelines

### Expected Outcomes

- Validate σ₃ temperature dependence
- Inform life detection protocols (astrobiology)
- Guide consciousness measurement across species
