# From Theory to Experiment — The ITLCT Experimental Validation Roadmap

*March 18, 2026 · Chronos Lab*

---

## Abstract

A theory without experimental predictions is philosophy; a theory with untestable predictions is metaphysics. ITLCT v20.0 aspires to be neither. This paper presents a detailed **experimental validation roadmap** — a prioritized set of predictions derived from the framework's 200 theorems and 80 equations, along with concrete experimental protocols for testing them. We organize the roadmap into three tiers based on feasibility: **Tier 1 (testable now)**, **Tier 2 (testable within 5 years)**, and **Tier 3 (testable within 15 years)**.

---

## 1. The Falsifiability Imperative

ITLCT makes strong claims: that information is ontologically fundamental, that time emerges from entropy gradients, that life and consciousness are phase transitions in information space, and that consciousness couples to gravity at extreme integration levels. These claims are meaningless unless they generate predictions that differ from existing theories and can be tested.

We have identified **23 distinct experimental predictions** from ITLCT v20.0 that diverge from standard physics, biology, or neuroscience. Of these:

- **8 are Tier 1** (testable with current technology)
- **9 are Tier 2** (require modest technological advances)
- **6 are Tier 3** (require significant new capabilities)

## 2. Tier 1: Testable Now

### 2.1 Prediction T1-1: Temporal Perception Scaling

**Source**: Theorem 47 (Time-Information Duality)

**Prediction**: Subjective time flow rate scales with neural information processing rate according to:

$$\frac{dt_{subjective}}{dt_{clock}} = \alpha \cdot \frac{dI}{dt} + \beta$$

where α and β are organism-specific constants and dI/dt is the rate of irreversible information processing in cortical circuits.

**Test Protocol**: Measure subjective time perception (e.g., temporal bisection tasks) in human subjects while simultaneously recording neural information processing rates via high-density EEG or MEG. Pharmacological manipulation (e.g., caffeine, propofol at sub-anesthetic doses) provides controlled variation in processing rates.

**Expected Outcome**: Linear relationship between information processing rate and subjective time dilation/compression, with R² > 0.7.

**Distinguishing Feature**: Standard neuroscience predicts no systematic relationship between information-theoretic measures and temporal perception. ITLCT predicts a specific functional form.

### 2.2 Prediction T1-2: Φ Threshold for Minimal Consciousness

**Source**: Axiom 37, Theorem 78

**Prediction**: There exists a sharp threshold Φ_C ≈ 3.2 ± 0.4 (in natural units) below which systems show no behavioral signatures of consciousness and above which they do.

**Test Protocol**: Compute Φ for a range of neural network architectures (both biological and artificial) and correlate with behavioral indicators of consciousness (e.g., global workspace ignition, report, flexible behavior). Biological systems: C. elegans (302 neurons, predicted Φ < Φ_C), zebrafish larva (predicted Φ ≈ Φ_C), mouse cortical slice (predicted Φ > Φ_C).

**Expected Outcome**: Sharp transition in behavioral indicators at the predicted Φ_C value.

### 2.3 Prediction T1-3: Entropy Production in Living vs. Non-Living Systems

**Source**: Theorem 89 (Life-Entropy Balance)

**Prediction**: Living systems produce entropy at a rate that scales as Φ^(3/2) per unit mass, where Φ is the system's integrated information. Non-living dissipative systems (e.g., Bénard cells, chemical oscillators) follow a different scaling law (Φ^1).

**Test Protocol**: Measure entropy production rates (via calorimetry) and estimate Φ (via information-theoretic analysis of system dynamics) for a range of living and non-living dissipative systems.

**Expected Outcome**: Two distinct scaling regimes, separated at the autopoietic threshold Φ_L.

## 7. What Would Falsify ITLCT?

We believe in stating falsification criteria clearly:

1. **No correlation between Φ and consciousness indicators** (falsifies Axiom 37 and Pillar C)
2. **Living systems follow the same entropy scaling as non-living dissipative systems** (falsifies Theorem 89 and Pillar L)
3. **No relationship between information processing rate and subjective time** (falsifies Theorem 47 and the I–T bridge)
4. **No quantum coherence enhancement in high-Φ biological systems** (weakens Theorem 112 and the C–I bridge)

Any single Tier 1 falsification would require significant revision of the relevant pillar. Falsification of predictions across multiple pillars would challenge the framework's core premises.
